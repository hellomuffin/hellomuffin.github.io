Chenhao Zheng

Email / Google Scholar / Twitter

I am a first year CS Ph.D. student at the University of Washington, advised by Prof. Ranjay Krishna. Before that, I was an undergraduate student at University of Michigan and Shanghai Jiao Tong University, where I was fortunate to also work with Prof. Andrew Owens and Dr. Adam Harley. I work on computer vision, with a particular interest in videos (video understanding, video->3d, video->robotics) and large vision-language models.

    Urban Rush: Making fitness fun with VR
    This project was done with Rahmy Salmon, Kalpit Haresh Sutariya, Akash Nallani and Ashish Patel. We develop immersive VR experiences aimed at encouraging exercise. Urban Rush lets the player to have fun in a narrative-based exploration while completing activities in a unique, procedurally generated city environment.
